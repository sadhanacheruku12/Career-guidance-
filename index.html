What Is Career Guidance?

Career guidance can be defined as a comprehensive, developmental program designed to assist individuals in making and implementing informed educational and occupational choices. In simple words, it is a journey on which people develop to make mature and informed decisions. It is the act of guiding or showing the way; it is the act of seeking advice.

Career guidance is the guidance given to individuals to help them acquire the knowledge, information, skills, and experience necessary to identify career options, and narrow them down to make one career decision. This career decision then results in their social, financial and emotional well-being throughout. Key elements of an effective career guidance program

There are several key elements of an effective career guidance program:
  • Professional counselor: A professional counselor is a person who can work with you to understand your career goals and use tested strategies on how you can achieve them. They often have formal education in counseling or certifications where they've learned best practices for this need.
  • Trusted environment: It's important to have a trusted environment where both the counselor and the person seeking help feel safe and trusted. This allows for a more realistic diagnosis of what the person might do and can increase motivation toward reaching career goals.
  • Shared goals: Career counselors have the goal of helping you with your unique need so it's important to clearly identify this early in the program. From there, you can set action items that can help you achieve it.
  • Schedule: Career programs often come with a specific schedule to help you reach your goals. This might include applying to a certain number of jobs within a time period or identifying by when you hope to pursue a new career.
